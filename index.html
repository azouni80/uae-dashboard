✈ Exit Routes from the UAE
OPTION A — AVAILABLE

DXB → International (Emirates / flydubai)

220+ combined departures/day (~20% pre-crisis). Emirates at 70% capacity — 150 flights to 125 destinations. flydubai 73 dep/day. NOTE: From 20 Apr, non-UAE airlines capped at 1 flight/day at DXB. Flexible rebooking through 31 May.

→ Book flexible exit tickets for your family on your preferred airline for the next 48-72 hours as insurance.

OPTION B — AVAILABLE

AUH → International (Etihad)

90-95 flights/day from Abu Dhabi. Etihad at ~84 daily departures to ~80 destinations — 60% of pre-crisis capacity. Gradual climb from 70-80 in March. Good backup if DXB flights cancel. Drive from Dubai ~1.5h.

OPTION C — OVERLAND BACKUP

Overland → Oman → Muscat (MCT)

Dubai → Hatta border: 1.5h. Hatta → Muscat: 3h. Total ~4.5h. Abu Dhabi → Khatm Al Shikla: ~5h. Borders open 24/7. Oman largely unaffected by strikes. International flights from Muscat available. Check your passport's Oman visa requirements before relying on this route.

⚠ WARNING: Mass exodus caused severe border delays at Hatta in mid-March. If situation escalates, leave at dawn.

OPTION D — AVAILABLE

Overland → Saudi Arabia → Riyadh/Jeddah

Abu Dhabi → Ghuwaifat border → Riyadh (~8h drive). International flights from Riyadh and Jeddah. Check Saudi visa requirements for your nationality.

NOT AVAILABLE

European Carriers (BA, Lufthansa, Air France/KLM)

ALL suspended to UAE (EASA conflict zone advisory CZIB 2026-03-R6). Next EASA review: 24 April. Air France earliest potential return: 3 May. KLM: 17 May. BA targeting 1 July restart. Lufthansa/Swiss: 31 May. Do not rely on European airlines for evacuation.

LAST RESORT

Private Charter

Available from Al Maktoum (DWC) and smaller airports. Cost: $200K-$250K to Europe. War risk insurance adds premium. Emergency only.

🌎 Important Notes for All Nationalities

  • Multiple countries have issued "AVOID ALL TRAVEL" advisories for the UAE, including the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, France, Netherlands, India, and more.
  • Register with your embassy: All team members should register with their respective embassies for evacuation notices and potential charter flights.
  • Check Oman visa requirements: Some nationalities get visa-free entry; others need an e-visa. Verify BEFORE relying on the overland route.
  • Check Saudi visa requirements: Varies by nationality. Verify transit/tourist visa eligibility.
  • Embassy charter flights: The US, UK, Canada, India, and other countries arranged government-funded evacuation flights in early March. Monitor your embassy for future charters.
  • Flexible tickets: Emirates and Etihad offer free rebooking through 30 April for tickets affected by the conflict.
